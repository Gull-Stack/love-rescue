The 4 Toxic Communication Patterns (Gottman's "Four Horsemen")

Dr. John Gottman spent 40 years studying couples in his "Love Lab." He discovered he could predict divorce with 94% accuracy by watching for just four communication patterns:

1. Criticism

Not the same as a complaint. Criticism attacks your partner's character, not their behavior. "You never think about anyone but yourself" vs. "I felt hurt when you forgot our plans."

2. Contempt

The most destructive pattern. Eye-rolling, mockery, name-calling, hostile humor. It communicates disgust and superiority. Gottman calls this the #1 predictor of divorce.

3. Defensiveness

Playing the victim, making excuses, cross-complaining. It tells your partner: "The problem isn't me, it's you." Nothing gets resolved.

4. Stonewalling

Withdrawing, shutting down, giving the silent treatment. Often happens when someone is emotionally flooded. The conversation dies â€” and resentment builds.
