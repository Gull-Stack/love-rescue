## THE 7 META-PATTERNS (Truths ALL Experts Converge On)

### 1. üîë SAFETY BEFORE EVERYTHING
Every single expert, without exception, says the same thing: **nothing works until the nervous system feels safe.**
- **Gottman**: Repair attempts only land when the listener isn't flooded (HR > 100 BPM = can't process)
- **Sue Johnson**: The 3 A.R.E. questions ‚Äî "Are you there? Can I reach you? Will you respond?" ‚Äî are safety checks
- **Chris Voss**: Tactical empathy calms the amygdala first, THEN the prefrontal cortex opens
- **Bren√© Brown**: Vulnerability requires psychological safety; shame thrives when safety is absent
- **Attachment Theory**: Secure base = safe haven. The entire attachment system is a safety-seeking mechanism
- **Tony Robbins**: State management ‚Äî you must regulate YOUR nervous system before engaging
- **Tatkin**: "Couple bubble" = mutual agreement to be each other's safe zone

**THE INSIGHT**: Safety isn't a feeling ‚Äî it's a neurobiological state. Every technique from every expert is either (a) creating safety or (b) only works once safety exists. The strategy engine should ALWAYS sequence safety-building before skill-building.

### 2. ü™û THE PRESENTING EMOTION IS NEVER THE REAL ONE
- **Sue Johnson**: Primary emotions (fear, longing, sadness) hide beneath reactive emotions (anger, criticism, shutdown)
- **Bren√© Brown**: "The story I'm telling myself is..." ‚Äî surface narrative conceals deeper shame/fear
- **Voss**: Getting to "That's right" requires reaching the emotion BEHIND the position
- **Gottman**: Criticism (surface) masks unmet needs (depth). "You never help" = "I feel alone and overwhelmed"
- **Attachment Theory**: Protest behaviors (anxious) and deactivating strategies (avoidant) are SECONDARY responses to primary attachment fear
- **Tony Robbins**: Every behavior is driven by one of 6 needs ‚Äî the behavior is the vehicle, not the need

**THE INSIGHT**: The #1 skill across all frameworks is EMOTIONAL ARCHAEOLOGY ‚Äî digging beneath the reactive emotion to find the vulnerable one. This should be a core assessment dimension and a daily practice in every strategy week.

---

### 3. üîÑ THE PURSUE-WITHDRAW CYCLE IS UNIVERSAL
- **Sue Johnson**: "Demon Dialogues" ‚Äî the pursue-withdraw pattern is THE central destructive cycle
- **Gottman**: Four Horsemen map directly: criticism/contempt = pursuit; defensiveness/stonewalling = withdrawal
- **Attachment Theory**: Anxious-avoidant trap ‚Äî hyperactivation vs. deactivation
- **Esther Perel**: Security vs. desire tension ‚Äî too much pursuit kills desire, too much distance kills safety
- **Voss**: "You're right" = withdrawal disguised as agreement. Mirroring/labeling breaks the cycle
- **Tony Robbins**: Significance-seeking (pursuit) vs. certainty-seeking (withdrawal)

**THE INSIGHT**: This is the SAME pattern described 6 different ways. The strategy engine should identify WHERE each person falls on the pursue-withdraw spectrum and tailor every single daily activity to their position. A pursuer needs to learn self-soothing; a withdrawer needs to learn reaching.

### 4. üìê THE 5:1 RATIO IS A UNIVERSAL LAW
- **Gottman**: 5:1 positive-to-negative ratio predicts relationship stability with 94% accuracy
- **Chapman**: Love tank metaphor ‚Äî constant deposits vs. withdrawals
- **Brown**: Shame resilience requires empathy encounters to outweigh shame encounters
- **Attachment Theory**: Earning secure attachment requires consistent positive bonding experiences
- **Robbins**: State management ‚Äî your baseline emotional state determines relationship quality
- **Voss**: Accusation audits FRONT-LOAD the negative so the positive has room to land

**THE INSIGHT**: Every framework has its own version of "you need overwhelmingly more positive than negative." This isn't just about conflict ‚Äî it's about the DAILY DEPOSIT RATIO. The strategy engine already tracks this but should weight it more heavily as THE leading indicator.

### 5. üéØ SPECIFICITY IS THE MECHANISM OF CHANGE

**THE INSIGHT**: The current strategy engine is ALREADY good at specificity in the solo flow. The matchup flow is too generic. Every strategy should have: exact words to say, exact duration, exact trigger, exact replacement behavior.

## SURPRISING TENSIONS BETWEEN EXPERTS

### 1. Security vs. Desire (Johnson vs. Perel)
- **Johnson**: Security IS the foundation of passion. The safer you feel, the more you can explore.
- **Perel**: Too much security KILLS desire. Desire needs mystery, otherness, risk.
- **RESOLUTION**: Both are right at different stages. Early/crisis couples need Johnson's security-first. Stable but bored couples need Perel's desire-through-distance. The strategy engine should detect which stage and prescribe accordingly.

### 2. Vulnerability vs. Strength (Brown vs. Robbins)
- **Brown**: Vulnerability is the birthplace of connection. Drop your armor.
- **Robbins**: State management ‚Äî you can control your emotional state in seconds. Be strong.
- **RESOLUTION**: Not contradictory ‚Äî they're sequential. Robbins' state management gets you CAPABLE of Brown's vulnerability. You regulate first, then open up from a place of strength, not collapse.

### 3. Individual Growth vs. Couple System (Perel vs. Gottman)
- **Perel**: You need to maintain your individual identity, mystery, separateness.
- **Gottman**: Build shared meaning, love maps, intertwined knowledge.
- **RESOLUTION**: The paradox of intimacy ‚Äî you need BOTH. Secure couples are deeply connected AND maintain individual selves. This is Tatkin's couple bubble with windows.
