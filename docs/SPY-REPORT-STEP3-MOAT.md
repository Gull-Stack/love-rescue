## Part 2: The Competitive Landscape — What Exists Today

### Practice Management (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane App)
- **What they do:** Scheduling, billing, notes, client portal, telehealth, insurance claims
- **Client portal:** Appointment booking, intake forms, secure messaging, document signing, payment
- **Couples support:** SimplePractice has "enhanced client management for couples" — creates joint profiles, shares billing. That's it.
- **What they DON'T do:** Zero between-session clinical tools. No assessments beyond generic screeners (PHQ-9, GAD-7). No relationship-specific insights. No partner dynamics. No homework tracking with depth.
- **Their moat:** Billing/insurance integration, HIPAA compliance, established user base (250K+ for SimplePractice)
- **Our opportunity:** They're plumbing. We're clinical intelligence. They complement us, not compete.

### Therapy Platforms (BetterHelp, Talkspace)
- **What they do:** Connect clients to therapists via text/video
- **Provider dashboard:** Client queue, session notes, messaging, scheduling
- **What they DON'T do:** No structured between-session work. No couple-specific assessments. No partner dynamics tracking. The therapist gets messages, not data.
- **Their moat:** Scale, brand recognition, therapist network
- **Our opportunity:** Their therapists are flying blind between sessions too. We give them instruments.

### Couples Apps (Lasting, Relish, Paired, Coral)
- **What they do:** Daily questions, lessons, exercises for couples
- **Therapist integration:** Essentially none. These are consumer-only products.
- **What they DON'T do:** No therapist dashboard. No clinical-grade assessments. No cross-framework analysis. No treatment plan integration.
- **Their moat:** Consumer brand, app store presence, simple UX
- **Our opportunity:** They're self-help. We're the bridge between self-help and clinical care.

### Assessment Tools (Gottman Checkup, Prepare/Enrich, ORS/SRS)
- **Gottman Checkup:** Gold standard for couples assessment. Therapist gets a detailed report. BUT: Gottman-framework only, static (one-time or periodic), no between-session tracking, no daily engagement.
- **Prepare/Enrich:** Pre-marital/enrichment assessment. Therapist-administered. One-time report. No ongoing tracking.
- **ORS/SRS (Outcome Rating Scale / Session Rating Scale):** 4-item screeners used session-to-session. Tracks trajectory but with zero depth — just "how are you feeling" on a slider.
- **Their moat:** Clinical validation, therapist training programs, institutional adoption
- **Our opportunity:** We can be Gottman Checkup + Prepare/Enrich + ORS/SRS + daily engagement + 5 more frameworks, all in one living system.
