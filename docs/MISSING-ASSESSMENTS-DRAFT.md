## 1. SHAME & VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT (Bren√© Brown)
### Purpose: Measure shame resilience, armor patterns, and vulnerability capacity

**Scale: 1-7 (Strongly Disagree ‚Üí Strongly Agree)**

#### Shame Triggers (8 questions)
1. When I make a mistake in my relationship, I feel like I AM the mistake ‚Äî not that I made one. [shame_identity]
2. I'm afraid that if my partner really knew everything about me, they wouldn't love me. [shame_fear_exposure]
3. When my partner criticizes me, I feel it as a judgment of my entire worth as a person. [shame_criticism]
4. I often compare my relationship to others' and feel like mine falls short. [shame_comparison]
5. When conflict arises, my first instinct is to prove I'm not the "bad one." [shame_defensiveness]
6. I feel ashamed of certain needs I have in my relationship (emotional, physical, or otherwise). [shame_needs]
7. I avoid bringing up issues because I'm afraid my partner will think less of me for having them. [shame_avoidance]
8. When I'm hurt, I'd rather hide it than show my partner I'm in pain. [shame_hiding]

#### Armor Patterns (8 questions)
9. I tend to try to be "perfect" in my relationship to avoid criticism or rejection. [armor_perfectionism]
10. When emotional pain gets too intense, I numb out ‚Äî with food, screens, work, or alcohol. [armor_numbing]
11. When things are going really well, I catch myself waiting for something to go wrong. [armor_foreboding_joy]
12. When something goes wrong, my first reaction is to figure out whose fault it is. [armor_blame]
13. I use humor or sarcasm to deflect when conversations get too emotionally intimate. [armor_deflection]
14. I keep myself busy enough that I rarely have to sit with uncomfortable feelings. [armor_busyness]
15. I present a curated version of myself to my partner rather than the full, messy truth. [armor_curation]
16. I'd rather be angry than sad ‚Äî anger feels safer. [armor_anger_shield]

#### Vulnerability Capacity (8 questions)
17. I can say "I'm scared" to my partner without feeling weak. [vuln_fear]
18. I can admit when I'm wrong without it destroying my sense of self. [vuln_admit_wrong]
19. I can ask for help when I need it without feeling like a burden. [vuln_ask_help]
20. I can express "I love you" first without needing to hear it back immediately. [vuln_express_love]
21. I can tell my partner when my feelings are hurt without attacking them. [vuln_express_hurt]
22. I can sit with uncertainty in my relationship without needing to control the outcome. [vuln_uncertainty]
23. I feel worthy of love without having to earn it through performance. [vuln_worthiness]
24. I can receive compliments and expressions of love without deflecting them. [vuln_receive]

#### "Story I'm Telling Myself" Awareness (4 questions)
25. When I'm triggered, I can recognize that the story in my head may not be the full truth. [story_awareness]
26. I can pause between a trigger and my reaction to check what I'm actually feeling underneath. [story_pause]
27. I've practiced saying "The story I'm telling myself right now is..." during conflict. [story_practice]
28. I can distinguish between what happened and the meaning I'm assigning to it. [story_distinguish]

## 2. SIX HUMAN NEEDS ASSESSMENT (Tony Robbins)
### Purpose: Identify dominant needs driving relationship behavior

**Format: Rank and rate scenarios (1-7 scale)**

#### Certainty (5 questions)
1. I feel most at ease in my relationship when I know exactly what to expect from my partner. [need_certainty_1]
2. Surprises in my relationship make me more anxious than excited. [need_certainty_2]
3. I need clear commitments and follow-through from my partner to feel safe. [need_certainty_3]
4. When plans change unexpectedly, it disrupts my sense of security. [need_certainty_4]
5. I prefer established routines over spontaneous changes in our relationship. [need_certainty_5]

#### Variety (5 questions)
6. I feel most alive when my partner and I try something completely new together. [need_variety_1]
7. Routine in my relationship makes me feel restless or bored. [need_variety_2]
8. I crave excitement and novelty ‚Äî doing the same thing repeatedly drains me. [need_variety_3]
9. I'm the one who usually suggests new experiences or adventures. [need_variety_4]
10. I sometimes create drama or conflict because it feels more alive than calm. [need_variety_5]

#### Significance (5 questions)
11. I need to feel like I'm the most important person in my partner's life. [need_significance_1]
12. I feel hurt when my contributions to the relationship go unrecognized. [need_significance_2]
13. Being respected and admired by my partner matters deeply to me. [need_significance_3]
14. I sometimes compete with my partner rather than collaborate. [need_significance_4]
15. I need to feel unique and irreplaceable in my relationship. [need_significance_5]

#### Connection/Love (5 questions)
16. I would sacrifice almost anything for a deep emotional connection with my partner. [need_connection_1]
17. Feeling emotionally close to my partner is more important than anything else. [need_connection_2]
18. I feel empty or lost when I sense emotional distance between us. [need_connection_3]
19. I sometimes say yes to things I don't want just to maintain closeness. [need_connection_4]
20. Physical touch and emotional intimacy are essential for me to feel loved. [need_connection_5]

#### Growth (5 questions)
21. I need to feel like I'm constantly evolving and getting better as a partner. [need_growth_1]
22. I feel frustrated when my relationship feels stagnant or stuck. [need_growth_2]
23. I value deep conversations about personal development with my partner. [need_growth_3]
24. I sometimes feel like my partner isn't growing at the same pace as me. [need_growth_4]
25. Learning new things together is one of the most important parts of our relationship. [need_growth_5]

#### Contribution (5 questions)
26. I feel most fulfilled when I know I'm making my partner's life better. [need_contribution_1]
27. Giving to my partner without expectation of return feels natural to me. [need_contribution_2]
28. I derive deep satisfaction from supporting my partner through difficult times. [need_contribution_3]
29. I sometimes give so much that I neglect my own needs. [need_contribution_4]
30. I feel a sense of purpose when I can positively impact my partner's well-being. [need_contribution_5]

## 3. DESIRE & ALIVENESS ASSESSMENT (Esther Perel)
### Purpose: Measure security/desire balance, individual identity, and erotic aliveness

**Scale: 1-7**

#### Security vs. Desire Balance (6 questions)
1. My relationship feels more like a safe harbor than an adventure. [desire_safe_harbor]
2. I sometimes feel like my partner and I have become more like roommates than lovers. [desire_roommates]
3. The comfort and predictability of my relationship sometimes feels like a trap. [desire_predictability_trap]
4. I miss the excitement and uncertainty of the early days of our relationship. [desire_miss_early]
5. I feel torn between wanting more closeness and wanting more independence. [desire_torn]
6. I sometimes feel more alive outside my relationship than inside it. [desire_alive_outside]

#### Individual Identity (6 questions)
7. I have passions and interests that are entirely my own, separate from my partner. [identity_own_passions]
8. I sometimes lose track of who I am outside of being a partner/spouse. [identity_lost_self]
9. My partner sees me as a confident, independent person, not just as their partner. [identity_seen_independent]
10. I regularly spend time doing things I love without my partner. [identity_solo_time]
11. I would describe myself as self-sustaining ‚Äî I don't need my partner to complete me. [identity_self_sustaining]
12. When I imagine my life, my individual dreams are as vivid as our shared dreams. [identity_individual_dreams]

#### Erotic Aliveness (6 questions)
13. I feel free to express my desires and fantasies with my partner. [erotic_free_expression]
14. I regularly feel physically desired by my partner ‚Äî not just loved. [erotic_feel_desired]
15. I can access a playful, uninhibited part of myself during intimate moments. [erotic_playfulness]
16. Our physical intimacy feels intentional and exciting, not routine. [erotic_intentional]
17. I can be fully present during intimate moments without my mind wandering. [erotic_presence]
18. I feel like my partner and I actively cultivate desire rather than waiting for it to happen. [erotic_cultivate]

#### "I Turn Myself On/Off When..." (6 questions)
19. I feel most drawn to my partner when I see them doing something they're passionate about. [turn_on_passion]
20. I feel desire fade when I take on too much of a caretaking role. [turn_off_caretaking]
21. I feel most alive and attractive when I'm investing in myself. [turn_on_self_investment]
22. I feel desire diminish when my relationship lacks novelty or surprise. [turn_off_no_novelty]
23. I feel most sexually alive when there's a sense of anticipation or longing. [turn_on_anticipation]
24. I notice my desire shutting down when I feel overwhelmed by responsibility. [turn_off_responsibility]

## 4. COMMUNICATION STYLE ASSESSMENT (Chris Voss / Tactical Empathy)
### Purpose: Measure listening quality, empathy accuracy, and communication effectiveness

**Scale: 1-7**

#### Listening Quality (6 questions)
1. When my partner is talking, I'm truly listening rather than planning my response. [listen_truly]
2. I can repeat back what my partner said before I respond with my own perspective. [listen_mirror]
3. I let my partner finish their complete thought before I start speaking. [listen_patience]
4. I ask questions to understand rather than to make a point. [listen_curious]
5. When my partner says something I disagree with, I try to understand their perspective first. [listen_disagree]
6. I notice myself mentally arguing with my partner while they're still talking. [listen_mental_argue] *reverse scored*

#### Empathy Accuracy (6 questions)
7. I can accurately name what my partner is feeling, even when they haven't said it directly. [empathy_name]
8. When my partner is upset, I focus on understanding their emotion before offering solutions. [empathy_feel_first]
9. I use phrases like "It seems like you're feeling..." to check my understanding. [empathy_labeling]
10. I can see a situation from my partner's perspective, even when I disagree. [empathy_perspective]
11. My partner would say I make them feel deeply understood. [empathy_partner_feels]
12. I can distinguish between what my partner is saying and what they're actually feeling underneath. [empathy_underneath]

#### Conflict Communication (6 questions)
13. In disagreements, I describe my own feelings rather than blaming my partner. [conflict_i_statements]
14. I can raise a concern without it sounding like an attack on my partner's character. [conflict_no_attack]
15. When I realize I'm wrong, I can say so without defensiveness. [conflict_admit_wrong]
16. I ask "How" and "What" questions rather than "Why" during conflict. [conflict_calibrated]
17. After an argument, I initiate repair ‚Äî I don't wait for my partner to make the first move. [conflict_repair]
18. I notice when my partner is flooding (overwhelmed) and suggest a break rather than pushing forward. [conflict_flooding_aware]

#### "That's Right" Skills (6 questions)
19. I can summarize my partner's position so accurately that they'd say "That's exactly right." [thatsright_summarize]
20. I validate my partner's experience even when I see it differently. [thatsright_validate]
21. I resist the urge to jump to "you're right" just to end a conversation. [thatsright_no_fake]
22. I can acknowledge my partner's pain without feeling like I'm admitting fault. [thatsright_acknowledge]
23. When my partner feels truly heard by me, I can feel the shift in the conversation. [thatsright_feel_shift]
24. I prioritize making my partner feel understood over making my point. [thatsright_priority]

## SCORING NOTES

### Shame Assessment
- **Shame Score** = average of Shame Triggers (higher = more shame)
- **Armor Score** = average of Armor Patterns (higher = more armored)
- **Vulnerability Score** = average of Vulnerability Capacity (higher = more open)
- **Story Awareness** = average of Story questions (higher = more aware)
- **Key Metric:** Vulnerability Score minus Shame Score = Resilience Gap (positive = resilient, negative = armored)

### Human Needs Assessment
- Rank the 6 needs by average score
- Top 2 = dominant needs (drive 80% of behavior)
- Flag unhealthy vehicle indicators (items ending in reverse-scored patterns)
- Cross-reference with attachment style for predictive insights

### Desire Assessment
- **Security-Desire Balance** = average of first section (higher = more security-oriented)
- **Identity Score** = average of identity section (higher = stronger individual identity)
- **Erotic Aliveness** = average of erotic section (higher = more alive)
- **Key Insight:** Low identity + low aliveness + high security = "roommate" pattern

### Communication Assessment
- **Listening Quality** = average of section 1
- **Empathy Accuracy** = average of section 2
- **Conflict Communication** = average of section 3
- **"That's Right" Skills** = average of section 4
- **Overall Tactical Empathy Score** = weighted average (empathy accuracy weighted highest)
