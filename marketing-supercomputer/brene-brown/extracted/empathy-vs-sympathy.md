# Empathy vs. Sympathy

## Core Principle
Empathy fuels connection. Sympathy drives disconnection. Empathy is feeling WITH people — climbing down into the hole with them. Sympathy is looking down from above and trying to silver-line their pain.

## Teresa Wiseman's Four Qualities of Empathy
1. **Perspective-taking** — recognizing their perspective as THEIR truth
2. **Staying out of judgment** — not easy when you enjoy it
3. **Recognizing emotion** in other people
4. **Communicating that recognition** — "I know what it's like down here. You're not alone."

## Empathy Requires Vulnerability
- To connect with someone's pain, you must connect with something in YOURSELF that knows that feeling
- It's a vulnerable choice — you're choosing to feel WITH them

## What Empathy Sounds Like
- ✅ "I don't even know what to say right now. I'm just so glad you told me."
- ✅ "I know what it's like down here, and you're not alone."
- ❌ "At least you..." (silver-lining)
- ❌ "Oh, it's bad, huh. Want a sandwich?"

## The "At Least" Trap
- "I had a miscarriage" → "At least you know you can get pregnant"
- "My marriage is falling apart" → "At least you have a marriage"
- We do this because difficult conversations make US uncomfortable
- We try to make it better → but connection is what makes it better, not solutions

## Quote
"Rarely, if ever, does an empathic response begin with 'at least.'"
