# Shame Resilience

## Core Principle
Shame — the fear that "I am not worthy of connection" — is the hidden driver behind most relationship dysfunction: defensiveness, perfectionism, numbing, contempt, withdrawal. Shame resilience is the ability to recognize shame, move through it, and maintain your sense of worthiness.

## Shame vs. Guilt
- **Shame:** "I AM bad" (identity-level)
- **Guilt:** "I DID something bad" (behavior-level)
- Guilt is adaptive — it motivates change. Shame is destructive — it paralyzes.

## Armor Patterns (How We Protect Against Shame)
1. **Perfectionism** — "If I look perfect, do it perfect, I can avoid shame"
2. **Numbing** — Addiction, food, alcohol, work, busyness
3. **Foreboding joy** — Refusing to feel joy because you're waiting for the other shoe to drop
4. **Blame** — Discharge pain onto others before sitting with it

## The Wholehearted Difference
- People who feel worthy of love don't avoid shame — they recognize it and talk about it
- "The less you talk about it, the more you have it"
- Courage = telling the story of who you are with your whole heart
- Compassion = being kind to yourself FIRST, then others

## Quote
"Is there something about me that, if other people know it or see it, that I won't be worthy of connection?"

## "The Story I'm Telling Myself"
- When triggered: pause and say "The story I'm telling myself right now is..."
- This creates distance between the trigger and your reaction
- Allows partner to correct the narrative before it spirals
