# Blame vs. Accountability

## Core Principle
Blame is the discharging of discomfort and pain. It has an INVERSE relationship with accountability. People who blame a lot seldom have the tenacity to hold people accountable, because they expend all energy raging for 15 seconds about whose fault something is.

## How Blame Works
- Something goes wrong → first instinct: whose fault is it?
- Speed: millisecond reaction. Brown's coffee story — cup drops, immediately "Damn you Steve!"
- Blame gives us a semblance of CONTROL. "I'd rather it be my fault than no one's fault."
- But blame is corrosive in relationships

## Blame vs. Accountability
| Blame | Accountability |
|-------|----------------|
| Discharge of pain/anger | Vulnerable process |
| "Whose fault is it?" | "My feelings were hurt about this" |
| 15-second rage | Sustained, honest conversation |
| Avoids vulnerability | Requires vulnerability |
| Corrosive | Connecting |

## Why It Destroys Empathy
- When listening to your partner's story, you're not really listening
- You're making connections as fast as you can about whose FAULT something is
- Miss the opportunity to empathize because you're too busy building a case

## Quote
"Blame is simply the discharging of discomfort and pain. It has an inverse relationship with accountability."

## LoveRescue Application
- **Assessment:** "When something goes wrong, what is your FIRST instinct? (a) Figure out whose fault it is (b) Understand what happened (c) Express how I feel (d) Fix it"
- **Daily Practice:** Catch yourself blaming → pause → replace with "I feel [emotion] about [situation]"
