# The Power of "No" & The Space Between Yes and No

## Core Principle
Stop trying to get people to say "yes." Yes means commitment, and commitment triggers fear. "No" means protection — and when people feel protected, they relax, think more clearly, and become MORE collaborative. There is far more space between yes and no than most people realize.

## Why "Yes" Is a Trap
- "Have you got a few minutes to talk?" → triggers: How long? Do I want to? Do I like you? How do I get off the phone?
- While processing all these questions, the person ISN'T LISTENING to you
- Getting people to say yes to small things to build to a big yes → now triggers suspicion ("where is this going?")
- Three kinds of yes: commitment, confirmation, and COUNTERFEIT. Most yeses are counterfeit.

## Why "No" Is Freedom
- "No" makes people feel protected and safe
- Protected people relax → their brain clears → they think 31% more effectively
- "Is now a bad time to talk?" → invites "no" → person says "No, no, it's fine — what's up?" (fully engaged)
- "Is it a ridiculous idea to...?" → invites "no" → which actually means "it's NOT ridiculous, let's talk"

## After the "No"
- Hesitate — give them 3 seconds (a "moment" for the brain to process)
- Stay pleasant — positive frame of mind makes the other person 31% smarter
- Their "no" + your pause + pleasant demeanor = they start generating solutions for you

## Application to Relationships
- Instead of: "Can we talk about our finances?" (triggers defensiveness)
- Try: "Would it be terrible if we looked at our budget together this weekend?" (invites "no")
- Instead of: "You need to help more around the house" (demands "yes"/compliance)
- Try: "Do you want me to burn out doing everything alone?" (invites "no")

## Quote
"We never call anybody on the phone and say 'have you got a few minutes to talk' — ever."
